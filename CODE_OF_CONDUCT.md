# Code of Conduct

_Community expectations for every Agent Radar space and contribution._

---

## 🤝 Our standard

We are committed to a welcoming, harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, or level of technical expertise.

Positive behavior includes:

- Showing empathy and respect
- Giving actionable, evidence-based feedback
- Accepting responsibility and learning from mistakes
- Focusing discussion on what is best for users and the community
- Crediting the work and ideas of others

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or deliberate misinformation
- Publishing another person's private information or credentials
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## 🛡️ Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, reject, hide, or remove contributions and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when behavior violates this standard. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and protect affected community members.

To report conduct privately, use GitHub's **Report a vulnerability** flow and prefix the title with `Conduct:`. If that option is unavailable, open a minimal issue requesting private maintainer contact without including sensitive details.

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and confidentially as practical. Retaliation against anyone making a good-faith report is prohibited.

## 🌍 Scope

This standard applies in the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and public spaces where someone represents Agent Radar.

This policy is inspired by the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), version 2.1.
